8.3 Water Use Natural availability of freshwater is affected by the occurrence and distribution of rainfall and the occurrence, movement and quality of surface water and groundwater. In Trinidad and Tobago, rainfall distribution is characterised by seasonal and geographical variation. Agard and Gowrie (2003) quantifies renewable water53 at 4.005 billion m3 per year for Trinidad, and 0.206 billion m3 per year for Tobago. Using the EVI scoring system it was shown that both Trinidad and Tobago have a very high demand on their water resources. This suggests therefore that the water resources should be carefully managed and that encroachment into watershed areas should be prevented in order to preserve their integrity.
