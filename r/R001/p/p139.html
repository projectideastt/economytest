6.2.4 Response The EMA promulgated the Water Pollution Rules to guide the quality of effluent discharged from industries into public drains, waterways and wetlands. These Rules are being amended and should become implementable in 2007. A National Hillside Development Policy, which updates and will supersede the Northern Range Hillside Development Policy 1988, is being prepared by a specialist Committee under the chair of the TCPD to regulate development on hillsides. A draft of this policy has been completed and submitted to TCPD for review. A programme of reforestation was established to replant areas which have been degraded by forest fires, mining, agricultural squatting and encroachment of urban development. The NRWRP is intended to replant approximately 33,000 ha of forest over a 10 year period. WASA has undertaken a major rehabilitation programme over the last few years to improve the output of some of its largest water treatment plants. These include an upgrade of the production capacity of the Caroni facility by 25%, expansion of the water impoundment area of the Navet Dam and increase in the production capacity of the North Oropouche facility to 20 million gallons per day. WASA is also continuing to update some of its operational processes such as making current its consumer database, establishing a full metering and leak detection system, and “…upgrading of its control system using supporting hydraulic modelling.” Water deficit is still experienced in many areas of the country including La Brea, parts of the East- West Corridor, and in south-west Tobago. In many areas of Laventille and San Juan there are constant public complaints about the lack of water, sometimes for weeks at a time.
