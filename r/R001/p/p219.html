Water Pollution Management Programme In 2005, a number of procedural documents were updated for administering the Water Pollution Registration and Permitting System These included:  User’s Guide Manual for Water Pollution Registration  User’s Guide Manual for Water Pollution Permitting  Public Guide for the Application of Registration of Water Pollutants Sources  Various application Forms along with Instructional Booklet.  Prototype Permits for Quarries and Sewage Treatment Plants

In addition, the EMA revised the development of the management processes and procedures that would be associated with the Water Pollution Rules once the Rules are made law. These instructional materials and informative exercises included:

 Development of an implementation strategy and action plan for the impending rules.  Costing for start-up and maintenance of both the Water Pollution Registration and Permitting including calculations for application fees and water pollution charges.  Experimental exercises with a select group of reviewers and the Water and Sewage Authority (WASA) in using the application forms associated with the draft rules to ensure clarity and applicability.  Development and testing of a management information system to store, update, retrieve, track and query all data collected from the water pollutant source registration system.  In-house meetings with EMA personnel to update and inform on rules and procedures.

Groundwater quality monitoring for MTBE and BTEX at elected sources in Trinidad and Tobago

In 2001, the EMA began a methodological assessment of groundwater quality in selected areas of Trinidad and Tobago. Initial studies involved surveys of temporary monitoring wells adjacent to selected service stations in Northern Trinidad. This assessment was initiated out of the need to evaluate the quality of ground water supplies used for potable water, with a view to better protecting human health and the environment, particularly due to increased use of Methyl Tertiary Butyl Ether (MTBE) as an alternative fuel additive coupled with the incidence of leaking underground fuel storage tanks at vehicle fuelling and service stations throughout the country. From November 2004 to April 2005, the EMA continued with its groundwater assessment programme expanding the study to include a wider coverage of Trinidad and Tobago focusing on the assessment of MTBE and BTEX in groundwater below selected service stations as well as potable water supplies managed by WASA. The final decision on which wells were to be included in the monitoring programme was based on accessibility to the well site, whether the wells were still intact and the location of the wells in relation to sites of known contamination. A total of eighteen production wells, seven observation wells and fourteen temporary monitoring wells were included in the final sampling programme. Sampling of potable water and ground water was performed in accordance with USEPA protocols. The six-month sampling programme of potable water and observation wells in WASA’s network and Temporary monitoring wells in EMA’s network allowed for some trends to be identified at key locations and for the identification of “hot spots” of pollution, which requires further monitoring and/or remediation.
