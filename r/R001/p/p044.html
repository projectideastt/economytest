Climate variability, enhanced greenhouse effect/global warming and stratospheric ozone are global issues of relevance to Trinidad and Tobago which are discussed in this chapter. There are now very few sceptics to the ‘reality of global climate change’ and there is strong evidence that human activities are the reason why this is happening. Changes in rainfall, temperature and sea level rise are the main concerns for Trinidad and Tobago. Collected data suggest that air temperature has been on the increase at least for the last 31 years.
