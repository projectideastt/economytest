A second ambient air quality monitoring programme was executed by the Town and Country Planning Division (TCPD). This project which included measurements of air quality parameters (nitrous oxides, sulphur oxides, volatile organic compounds, total suspended particulates [TSP] of aerodynamic diameter 10 µm [PM10] or 2.5 µm [PM2.5], nitrates in TSP, sulphate in PM2.5 and metals in PM10) was conducted over a four-week period in Point Lisas (two stations) and Chaguaramas (one station). Except for a single measurement of TSP in the Point Lisas central area which exceeded the United States Environmental Protection Agency's (USEPA's) 24-hour average of 150 µgm-3, the levels of all the other reported parameters were within accepted international ambient standards. No explanation was provided for this single discrepancy. In addition, there have been several pollutant inventory studies done to characterise air pollution sources and estimate loads, some of which used simplified models (UNDP/EMA 1998, EMA 2001a) and one based on actual consumption data (EMA 2001b). Apart from these specific pollutant inventory studies, the EMA has established an investigation and complaints desk since 1995, and has compiled and categorised data on air quality complaints from 1995 to 2000. From the 2000 analysis of the data it can be concluded that air pollution is an area of major concern for the public, with two activities which stand out, namely autobody repair and mechanics, and woodworking (Figure 3.20).
