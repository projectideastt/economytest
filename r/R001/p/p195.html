8.4.4 Response A number of measures are currently in effect or proposed which focus on the supply side of the transportation equation. These are geared at improving the current traffic situation and to allow for easier movement of people and freight. These measures include the upgrading of the existing road network,

improvement in the public transportation system and the introduction of a mass transit rail system. However, given our land space limitations and the need to ensure environmental sustainability other measures are also necessary to curb the growth in demand. Transportation Planning A National Transportation Plan is currently being prepared for Trinidad and Tobago. This comprehensive study and plan, which was commissioned in 2004, attempts to address the sea, air and land transportation needs of the country for the next 30 years30. However, the results of this planning process have not yet been made public. Upgrading the Nation’s Road Infrastructure The policy objective of the Government is to develop a road network to ensure that within Trinidad no destination will be more than two and half hours away. As a result, the Budget Statements of the last three years proposed a number of major road projects, all geared at extending and improving the East- West and North-South Corridors to improve capacity and safety and opening up new road arterials: • Extension of the Solomon Hochoy Highway to Point Fortin; • Construction of a spur that parallels the SS Erin Road to cover areas from Debe to Erin; • Construction of a highway from San Fernando to Mayaro, via Princes Town and Rio Claro; • Construction of a highway from Port-of-Spain to the Chaguaramas Peninsula; • Construction of an Eco Roadway on the North Coast from Matelot to Blanchisseuse; • Extension of the Churchill Roosevelt Highway to Manzanilla via Sangre Grande;
