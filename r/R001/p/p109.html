In 2003, 96% (or 10,994.4 tonnes) of the hazardous waste generated was distributed among 189 companies in Trinidad (Figure 4.3). Only 4%, approximately 458.1 tonnes, was generated in Tobago by 96 companies (Figure 4.3). It should be noted however that several companies, mainly from the tourism sector, did not participate in the inventory. The largest companies which were the major generators of hazardous waste displayed greater environmental awareness and had systems in place to treat and dispose of hazardous waste in a responsible manner. 4.5.4 Response Little attempt has been made to reduce the amount of waste generated. In 1989, SWMCOL established waste recovery systems and markets for recyclable materials, including glass, paper, cardboard, metals, used-oil and textiles. The establishment of a recycling industry was meant to reduce the amount of waste entering landfills, while at the same contribute to the conservation of resources, employment creation and income generation, and to create a source of foreign exchange. However, the recycling industry remains largely underdeveloped. Most of the sorting is done at the landfills creating health problems for the persons who sort through the garbage to collect recyclable materials.

In recent times, the Government has been considering various options for improving management of the country’s waste. One such option is the use of plasma-based systems, which utilise ionised gas at a high temperature (as high as 10,000 ºC) to convert waste to a vitrified substance with separation of molten metal. The technology converts waste into slag and significantly reduces its volume and weight with electrical energy as a by-product. However, this technology requires a high level of expertise and energy for proper functioning. SWMCOL issued a public call for tenders for a waste disposal system for Trinidad. The proposer is expected to address the treatment and disposal of waste including some aspects of hazardous waste. This tender is currently being evaluated. An earlier contract for design and implementation of a waste collection system is currently underway and the successful bidder of the waste disposal system will be expected to work with the existing contractor. Meanwhile, the municipal authorities are considering extending the term of contract from one to three years for existing contractors engaged in the collection and haulage of garbage.
