ORGANISATION STRUCTURE

The Authority is governed by a Board of Directors comprising nine members and a Chairman, appointed by the President of the Republic of Trinidad and Tobago. The Managing Director/CEO, who is appointed by the Board, is an ex-officio member of the Board, while the Manager, Corporate Services serves as the Corporate Secretary. The Board reports to the Minister with responsibility for the Environment. The Members of the Board and Board Committees are detailed in Appendix 1.

The Authority itself is organized into five (5) responsibility centres:

CEO’s Office Oversees the implementation of the policies and priorities of the Board and work programmes of the Authority as well as the co-ordination with other governmental entities and regional and International organisations. Preparation of the Annual Report also falls under this office.

The Human Resource Unit The Human Resource Unit falls under the CEO’s office and is responsible for the following: • Manpower Planning • Succession Planning • Recruitment / Selection • Performance Management • Training • Industrial Relations • Compensation and Benefits • Employee Relations

The Legal Services Department Responsible for the following: • Internal and External Instructing Services • Advising the Technical Services Department • Litigation - Preparation of Court Documents • Mediation/Alternate Dispute Resolution • Drafting of Legislation/Amendments

A critical aspect of the work of this department involves translating, into legal form, technical instructions from the Technical Services Department. Internal instructing services are achieved through promoting an effective communication Network within the EMA and specifically between the Legal Services and the Technical Services Department. The Legal Services Department, in addition to making recommendations for draft legislation, is also actively engaged in the exercise of amending the Environmental Management Act and other pieces of legislation with direct bearing on environmental laws e.g. Summary Offences Act.

Corporate Relations And Public Education Department Responsible for ensuring that the Authority establishes itself as a continuing presence and influence in the nation’s consciousness. In focusing on environmental improvement, the Corporate Relations and Public Education Department develops special education initiatives to change attitudes and behaviours towards Air, Water, Noise, Waste disposal and our Ecosystem. Its education programmes target various populations in schools and communities and often utilize print and electronic media.
