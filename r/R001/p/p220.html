The shallow groundwater below the NP Service Station at Maraval Road, Port-of-Spain, was still contaminated although the Underground Storage Tanks (USTs) at this station were removed in 2003 by NP. The shallow groundwater below service stations in Scarborough and Canaan in Tobago as well as Charlieville, Central Trinidad, also indicated that the service stations in these areas were in need of upgrade and that site remediation is required.

Among the potable water and observation wells in WASA’s network, only three “hot spots” were identified at Four Roads in the Diego Martin Producing Field, at Curepe in the Valsayn Producing Field and at Don Miguel Road, which is the offtake for the El Socorro Gravels. These wells require further routine monitoring and the service stations upgrade of these well should be inspected.

Hazardous Waste Inventory Study

Trinidad and Tobago is a signatory to the Basel Convention on the Transboundary shipment of Hazardous Wastes. As at 2005, Trinidad and Tobago has not yet reported on the volumes of waste generated. In order to keep up with international obligations, the Government of Trinidad and Tobago embarked on an inventory study to meet this requirement.

The EMA is co-managing this project, which was launched in August 2005 and set for completion in early 2006, and it is expected to generate data on hazardous waste volumes and categories. This information will be used to formulate strategies and policies for hazardous waste management.

Designation of Environmentally Sensitive Species (ESSs) The Pawi, the Manatee and the White-tailed Sabre-wing hummingbird were identified for designation as ESSs in 2005, with the following achievements:  Administrative records for the three ESSs were sent out for public comment from January- February 2005.  Notices were signed on World Environment Day- June 5, and gazetted on June 17, Legal Supplement Part B – Vol. 44, No. 98 - June 17, 2005.  Legal Notices 123 (Manatee), 124 (Pawi), 125 (Sabre-wing hummingbird).

In addition to the above, five species of Marine Turtles were identified for designation as ESSs. The five marine turtle species selected, Leatherback, Green, Hawksbill, Olive Ridley and Loggerhead are listed as globally endangered species by CITES, the SPAW Protocol and under the IUCN Red List. Draft Schedules for the five species were sent out to 30 stakeholder agencies with which the EMA has memoranda of understanding. At present, conflicting local legislation on marine turtles is delaying designation.

Launch of the Strategic Environmental Assessment Unit to Trinidad and Tobago’s Energy Sector

Energy Sector Stakeholders were presented in February 2005 with the two-year work plan for the Strategic Environmental Assessment Unit as it related to energy sector matters. The EMA used this forum as an opportunity to solicit feedback from stakeholders for use in future planning. It was also an opportunity to network and establish valuable relationships with the Energy Stakeholders.
