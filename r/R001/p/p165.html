Threatened and Vulnerable Species A large number of threatened species reflects the vulnerability of a country’s biodiversity. There are very few studies on the threatened species in Trinidad and Tobago. Labelling a species as rare or threatened is based on local knowledge and not necessary based on scientific assessment. As a result, the information on threatened species is highly variable. The World Conservation Union (IUCN) Red List of Threatened Species, documents 37 threatened animal species, but carries no corresponding listing for plant species41. The manatee or sea cow, green turtle, river otter, pawi, and blue and yellow macaw are among those listed as endangered. Table 7.9 shows the current listed threatened animal species in Trinidad and Tobago as categorised by the 2006 IUCN Red List in relation to other Caribbean countries. In contrast, Agard and Gowrie (2003) noted the total number of threatened species as 147 and total number of endangered species as 16. The Wildlife Section of the Forestry Division provides a 2005 list of 20 endangered, 10 vulnerable and 18 rare species (Box 3). Exploited Species Trinidad and Tobago’s species are mainly exploited for commercial and recreational purposes and for home use.
