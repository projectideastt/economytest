WASA has indicated that it is “…on track to achieve the United Nations Millennium Development goals of ensuring environmental sustainability and adequate access to safe drinking water and improved sanitation by 2015” (Grimes 2005) and its programme of development includes the development of new production wells, rehabilitation and refurbishing of service reservoirs and booster stations and installation of new pipes at the rate of 100 km per year. A number of complementary projects are being pursued by the Ministry of Agriculture; the Ministry of Community Development, Culture and Gender Affairs (MCDCGD); and the MPUE and include: the establishment of a national reforestation and watershed rehabilitation programme; a community environmental protection and enhancement programme; acquisition of contract services for design and implementation of a solid waste collection programme; and the soon to be acquired services for the development of a solid waste disposal facility. Finally, WASA established a new waste water facility close to the Beetham Landfill and has made some gains in water availability with operation of the desalinisation plant at Point Lisas.
