5.5.2 Pressure The pressures associated with coastal erosion relate to the loss of property, resource use and infrastructure. A useful example of the latter is that observed along the Manzanilla Bay in Trinidad where the single coastal road between two towns and connecting several villages, came within immediate threat of damage through coastal erosion. The threat was easily observed by passers-by, but remained unattended for several years. Similar situations have occurred near Point Fortin and Cedros where, not

only is infrastructure threatened but so too property including crops and other agricultural supported chattel such as machinery and equipment, livestock and/or commodities. Coastal erosion, in some areas, has also impacted the use of beaches whether as sea-bathing places (recreational use) or for the mooring and landing of small local fishing vessels and their catch (commercial use). To date such loss is restricted to Trinidad’s south western peninsula. 5.5.3 Condition and Trends Expanding the land seaward is likely to continue as demand is escalating all types of coastal development. These include commercial, residential and industrial development projects. Of these, the many high rise luxury apartments stand out. These new coastal developments are expected to join the existing ones such as Westmoorings, Gulf View, MovieTowne and Gulf City. Upgrading or reconstruction of the main transit through the area, i.e. the Western Main Road from Cocorite to Chaguaramas, is expected to be executed soon. The expanding demand for more port facilities at Port Point Lisas and the new demand at Union Village will continue to encourage reclamation works or pier construction. Both are likely to impact the marine nearshore but in different ways. Moreover, the Government’s programme of locating gas-based industries along the west coast of Trinidad will require greater quantities of cooling water which will have the potential to induce change in the nearshore marine environment. 5.5.4 Response Response to coastal erosion can be thought of as relating to three categories: the preparation and institution of regulations; the State’s participation in development projects; and the establishment of monitoring schemes.
