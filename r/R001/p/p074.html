The Plan was prepared to implement the halon phase-out schedule under the Montreal Protocol. The use of Halon 1211 has almost been eliminated and so this phased Plan at the time provided a strategy which addresses the continued consumption of Halon 1301 and enables Trinidad and Tobago to: • Reduce and eventually stop the emissions of halons into the atmosphere. • Educate the public about the negative effects of halons on the environment. • Identify essential and non-essential uses of halons while seeking adequate alternatives for essential uses. • Recover all halons from non-essential uses and distribute only for essential uses. • Achieve the targeted phase-out programme for halons in Trinidad and Tobago. The plan calls for the establishment of the Bank to manage stocks of halons to meet the country’s needs up until 2005 when their use will be terminated.
