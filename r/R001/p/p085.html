Figure 4.2: Economic Geology of Trinidad and Tobago

(Source: MEEI; pers. comm. 2006)

Both oil and natural gas are being discovered in new fields off the north and northeast coasts of the country, while land-based oil and gas continue to be exploited on a reduced scale, particularly in Penal, La Brea and Point Fortin. Between October 2000 and May 2001, land-based crude oil production was roughly 23% of the nation’s eight month production of 27.2 million barrels. For the period October 2004 to May 2005, some four years later, the share of the country’s land based crude oil production was approximately 18% of the 32.2 million barrels. While overall production had increased over the same eight month period, the share of land-based production had decreased by roughly 4%.
