The area under cultivation of tree crop, non-tree crop, ornamentals, etc. is 47,903 ha, of which 6,839 ha comprise tree crops and 29,689 ha consist of non-tree crops in both pure and inter-planted stands (Table 4.13). Tree crops consist of non orchards and orchards, and for the former, cocoa and coconut, together accounted for 76% of the land cultivated. Oranges were the single largest orchard crop cultivated and accounted for 54% of the land cultivated.

Of the 29,689 ha of crop land under cultivation of non-tree crops, crops in pure stands accounted for 21,017.2 ha or approximately 71% while inter planted stands consisted of 8,671.5 ha or 29% (Table 4.14). The category ‘Other Crops’ accounted for the single largest area of land under cultivation, and sugar cane cultivation consumed 14,917.9% or roughly 97% of the cultivated land.
