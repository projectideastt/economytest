ACCOMPLISHMENTS FOR 2005

CORPORATE RELATIONS AND PUBLIC EDUCATION DEPARTMENT

COLLABORATIVE PROJECTS

Anti-Litter Campaign  The EMA collaborated with TIDCO, the city and Borough Corporations of Port of Spain, San Fernando, Chaguanas and Arima, as well as members of the private sector, to place temporary bins along Carnival band routes before the Carnival weekend. The project, “Throw Your Waste in a Bin”, was complemented by television and radio advertisements.

Forest Fire Awareness and Prevention  The EMA partnered with the Forestry Division to promote its Forest Fire Awareness and Prevention programme. A Forest Fire publicity and education programme was undertaken to sensitise the public to safe practices during the Dry Season.

Best Village Environmental Competition  The EMA served on the Executive Committee of the Best Village Environmental and Sanitation Competition, an activity spearheaded by the Ministry of Community Development, Culture and Gender Affairs. This competition, which forms part of the overall Prime Minister’s Best Village Competition, is designed to encourage community residents across Trinidad and Tobago to come together and work towards the improvement, regeneration and maintenance of their local environments.

Tidy T&T  The EMA continued to work with Tidy T&T, a joint project of the Rotary Clubs in Trinidad and Tobago. This is a national environmental improvement initiative intended to benefit all communities by capturing the popular imagination and encouraging an increased awareness and sensitivity of environmental issues.

SCHOOLS’ PROGRAMMES The year 2005 marked the seventh year of competition for both the Primary Schools’ ‘Hands-on’ and the Secondary Schools’ Public Speaking programmes. The Department has also expanded its programmes to include private schools, as well as schools in Tobago.

Lectures and Displays  Throughout 2005, the Public Education (PE) unit continued its lectures, workshops and displays for the nation’s schools, NGOs, CBOs, Faith-Based Organisations (FBOs), industrial and business companies and other civil society groups.

Climate Change Project  The EMA participated in Education and Public Awareness initiatives of the Mainstreaming Adaptation to Climate Change (MACC) project, which involves 12 participating CARICOM member states. The principal objective of the MACC is to build capacity within CARICOM to develop adaptation strategies and measures, according to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC).

 The PE unit also participated in a Climate Change workshop in Uruguay. This workshop was organised for countries in Latin America and the Caribbean to address issues under Article 6 of the Convention. (Article 6 on the Convention deals with Education, Public Awareness and Training.)
