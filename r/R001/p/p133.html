This situation is further exacerbated by increased sectoral demands and conflicts that have arisen with respect to competing demands between residential and commercial, mostly hotel use. DHV Consultants et al. (1999) reported that the domestic sector is the largest single user of water in the country, accounting for approximately 36% of demand; followed by the industrial sector accounting for 18%, and irrigated agriculture accounting for only approximately 3% of demand. Unaccounted-for water comprises 43% of water demand. Although difficult to quantify, ecological demand represents an important user of water (Figure 6.2) since water is necessary to maintain the productive ecology of Trinidad and Tobago’s rivers and wetlands. 6.2.2 Pressures The rivers are under pressure as they receive a range of surface water runoff with a wide array of chemicals from agricultural, industrial and sewerage plants, solid waste and urban development.
