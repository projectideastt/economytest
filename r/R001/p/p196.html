• Extension of the Diego Martin Highway to Diamond Vale; • Construction of the interchange at the intersection of the Churchill Roosevelt and Uriah Butler Highways; • Construction of interchanges or overpasses at key intersections along the East-West Corridor, such as Aranguez, El Soccoro, Curepe, Macoya and Trincity. In addition, a roads concessionaire system is also proposed where developers would design, finance, build, maintain, and operate specific highways on behalf of the Government. Light Rail Mass Transit System Priority is also being given to the development of a Light Rail Mass Transit System which is expected to form the foundation of Trinidad’s transport system. A full feasibility study has been completed and on this basis, a decision was made to proceed with the system. The system will traverse over 120 km and serve over two-thirds of Trinidad’s population, and will link Port of Spain and San Fernando, as well as several town centres including Arima, Diego Martin, and Sangre Grande. Some segments of the rail system, those from Curepe to Port of Spain and from Curepe to Chaguanas, are expected to be in place by the year 2010. Sea Transportation Sea transportation to and from north and south Trinidad is also seen as an alternative to road transportation. Proposals for a water taxi system from Point Fortin to Carenage are currently being conceptualised. Travel Demand Management In 2005, licences were granted for dealers to import 21,860 cars. The MTI has since established an allocation system to limit the quantities of used motor cars imported into the country.
