4.5.3 Current State and Trends Solid Waste The responsibility for solid waste management and disposal is shared by a number of agencies and therein lies part of the problem. SWMCOL was established in 1980, to be the national solid waste authority with responsibility for management of the solid waste systems in the country. The regional governing bodies are responsible for collection of domestic waste within their individual municipality. Moreover, because there is no one institution/agency responsible, there is often duplication of effort in some areas and neglect in others. For various administrative and technical reasons, the collection and haulage of solid waste in the municipalities is an inefficient and ineffective operation. Collection scheduling problems are the main difficulties. Residents are often unaware of collection schedule changes or simply ignore scheduled pickups, opting to place household waste outside of scheduled pickups. In addition, all businesses have a legal responsibility for the waste they produce. However, these responsibilities are usually neglected, particularly in the commercial sector. The municipal authorities are obliged to dispose of the waste of a commercial enterprise within a limited weight. Beyond this weight, it is the responsibility of the enterprise to dispose of its own waste. Yet some businesses do not adhere to this arrangement. There is little or no regulation of the operations of the country’s landfills. The three public landfills, all of which have passed the average 20 year lifespan recommended for operation of a landfill, operate at low levels of efficiency and pose health and security risks to persons entering and operating in the facilities. Their operations are also hampered by low resources. While solid materials, surface run-off and leachate from wastes can have devastating effects on marine ecosystems and can contaminate freshwater and groundwater supplies, there is no consistent and regular monitoring of these waste facilities. In recent times concerns have been voiced about the Guanapo and Beetham facilities as sources of contamination which affect the groundwater and freshwater supplies.

Safe handling of wastes at the facilities still presents a challenge since salvagers are known to indiscriminately sort and handle waste materials, and at times even unearth buried materials.
