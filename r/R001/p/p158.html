Coral Reef In Tobago, reef systems include Buccoo Reef (the only protected reef), Flying Reef, the Speyside Reef, Man-of-War Bay Reef, Culloden Bay Reef, and Arnos Vale Reef.

In Trinidad: there is the Salybia Bay Reef Coral reefs protect the coastline from erosion, storm surge and tsunamis. They also act as nurseries for commercial fish species, such as snappers, conch and lobsters, and for other fish species thus serving as important means through which fishermen can earn a livelihood, and as significant sources of food. Reefs are important recreational and aesthetic attractions forming the basis of dive tourism, providing employment and income for reef boat and dive tour operators and others. The reefs of southwest Tobago contribute to the white sandy beaches in that part of the region. Coral reefs are affected by impacts of land-based activities, of which the main ones include:  Overgrowth of algae due to nutrient enrichment;  Chronic oil toxicity;  Water turbidity as a result of dredging operations;  Smothering due to elevated sediment inputs;  Over-fishing;  Excessive visitation levels;  Destruction from reef walking and coral harvesting; and  Boat anchor damage.
