However, the section cautions that these figures do not reflect the number of protected species in captivity since some persons do not comply with this licensing requirement. The species listed under CITES49 require a licence for export, import, re-export and introduction into a new area. Under this Convention, the Wildlife Section issued 80 permits in 2003/2004 to export species, the majority of which included the orange-winged parrots exported for personal use and other animals such as lizards, scorpions, spiders, bats and frogs. 7.3.4 Response Prohibited Areas Arresting the decline of, or increasing population levels of threatened and vulnerable species and securing their habitat and food sources are critical measures towards species protection. The declaration of the 13 wildlife sanctuaries to date is geared mainly at prohibiting hunting in these ‘sacred’ areas but also presents the opportunity for the protection of habitat and food supply. However, the declaration of these areas appears to focus on the protection of bird species. Of the 13 sanctuaries, 6 are dedicated to the protection of nesting or roosting bird species (Table 7.13), with 5 of these areas being small islands, islets and rocks where breeding colonies of seabirds are to be protected from poaching and hunting50. Of the 7 remaining wildlife sanctuaries, 3 are located in the hilly and mountainous terrain, also representing a geographical spread, that is, the Northern Range, Central Range, and Southern Range.
