Agricultural Squatting The practice of agricultural squatting is widespread. As recently as 1998, the Government had identified the existence of some 255 unauthorised settlements on lands owned by the State or other government agencies. South Trinidad had 132 squatter settlements of which 71 were located on lands owned either by Caroni (1975) Limited or Palo Seco Agricultural Enterprises Limited. Additionally, 22 squatter settlements were identified in central Trinidad, 98 in north Trinidad and 3 in Tobago. According to the 2004 Agricultural Census, some 8.4% or 7,125 ha of the nation’s agricultural land was occupied by squatters as of 2004 (CSO 2005c). Table 4.15 which is abstracted from Table 5-13 of the 2004 Agricultural Census Report, identifies the number of parcels and the size of the areas of government, State agencies and privately owned lands currently occupied by squatters.
