Because of its importance as a water source, the Caroni watershed represents one of the key areas where detailed studies have taken place. Data from previous reports illustrate the pollution problem in northwest Trinidad (Table 6.2). A 31 day study of the Caroni River at the Caroni Arena Water Treatment Plant (CAWTP) in 1997 estimated pollutant loads of 5,713 tonnes with a daily average of 184 tonnes. Most of the pollutants (88%) were derived from high sediment loads, with organic matter and organic contaminants, each accounting for five percent. The Mausica/Manacal and Arima Rivers were heavily polluted and were rated fairly poor in water quality, while the Guanapo River was rated as having poor water quality (EMA 1997). It is estimated that about half the water won by WASA is lost through a combination of leaking pipes and fire hydrants, illegal connections, wastage and unregistered use (Water Technology 2006). Less than 30% of the nation’s population has an uninterrupted (24 hours/7 days per week) supply and approximately 8% has no access to piped water services (Water Technology 2006).

6.2.3 Current State and Trends Many of the rivers in the country have lost their vigour as they are being slowly starved of water from their sources. They are often filled with sediments which, over time, reduce river widths.
