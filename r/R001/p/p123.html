5.4.2 Pressure The fishery resources are said to be over exploited in the inshore areas but under exploited offshore. Additionally, pollution in the near shore waters is taking a toll on habitats such as coastal wetlands, seagrass beds and reefs, which provide protection and food for juvenile organisms. Table 5.5 provides an overview of the state of some selected species of fish and shellfish. Depleting resources are also a consequence of overfishing by fishermen in the inshore waters and illegal fishing in the offshore waters, along with problems such as land based pollution from sewage and agricultural chemicals.
