TECHNICAL SERVICES DEPARTMENT

Lead Remediation at two sites in East Trinidad This will represent follow up to work done by the EMA in 2000 at Demerara Road and La Chance Trace, Arima. These activities include compiling detailed information obtained from site investigations, assessments and sampling to facilitate decision-making on the mechanism of remediation. Since start up in January 2005, a significant portion of work was completed at Demerara Road and due to weather conditions and other factors it will be continuing in 2006.

This project has faced challenges including waterlogged conditions due to heavy rainfall, the need to remove and relocate of wildlife present at Demerara Road, change in project scope to utilise ex situ remediation instead of in situ remediation as was initially proposed, the need to source a disposal site for the treated waste and firm decision on the future course of action for La Chance Trace residents. Due to the challenges mentioned above, the project remains incomplete.

Designation of Environmentally Sensitive Areas (ESAs) The Aripo Savannas, the Buccoo Reef and the Nariva Swamp were prioritized for designation as ESAs in 2005. Procedures for stakeholder and public consultations were executed and the following were achieved:  Administrative Record for Aripo Savannas sent out for public comment from March-May 2005  Draft Schedules for Nariva Swamp was sent out to stakeholder agencies from May-June 2005  Nariva Swamp Administrative Record put out for public comment from June–July 2005 Designation of ESAs faced a significant setback because boundary descriptions have delayed the process. There were changes in boundary descriptions for Nariva Swamp and Aripo Savannas. In addition, the High Water Mark definition for the Buccoo Reef remains unresolved.

Waste Management - Lead Remediation Projects and the Hazardous Waste Inventory initiated in previous years are to continue in 2006.

Healthy Ecosystems – Designation of five species of marine turtles, research in the Matura National Park, upgrade of the Aripo Management Plan and the development of species recovery plans for the Sabre-wing Humming Bird and the Pawi were started in 2005 and are expected to be completed in 2006.

Environmental Assessment Unit - The scoping phase for a Strategic Environmental Assessment for the North Coast Marine Area (NCMA) will be initiated in 2006. Capacity building exercises will be conducted to boost the ability of the EMA and its team of local external reviewers in reviewing Environmental Impact Assessment (EIAs). Developmental work on the CEC Database is expected to occur in 2006.

Geographic Information Systems (GIS) - The EMA expects to complete its GIS Needs Assessment in 2006.

Structural Changes - In 2006, the EMA expects to establish a formalised unit for the monitoring of CEC Certificates. Currently, this function in managed as part of the work carried out by the Compliance Unit which deals primarily with environmental incidents and complaints.
