On the north-east side, the headlands and beaches are smaller and more numerous. The east coast is characterised by low lying areas and the longest stretches of beaches and bays such as Matura, Cocos and Mayaro are separated by three headlands at Matura, Manzanilla and Mayaro. In Tobago, the southwest coast is made up of a flat limestone platform which is approximately 20 m in depth and possibly extends seaward to form the coral reefs. This area contains large expanses of beaches, a lagoon, and a coral reef complex that is the largest in Trinidad and Tobago. The north coast, east of Plymouth, is characterised by a series of headlands and bays with Castara, Parlatuvier and Charlotteville being among the most prominent. On the southeast coast the bays at Speyside and Roxborough are the most well-known.
