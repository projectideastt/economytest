Apart from impacts due to declining water quality, wetlands, coral reefs and seagrasses have been physically destroyed to make way for changes in resource use and other human activities, for example: the removal of seagrass mainly for built development; the in-filling and reclamation of mangroves and swamps for residential, commercial and industrial purposes; and destruction of coral by reef walking during visitor tours. 7.2.2 Current State and Trends The extent and quality of the country’s terrestrial ecosystems can be used to define changes in habitat, and thus give some indication of their biodiversity status.
