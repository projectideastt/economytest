The latter, and specifically its Fisheries Division is charged, among others, with managing and conserving the nation’s marine resources and executing the various regulations consistent with the Fisheries Act (Chapter 67:51), as well as upholding the various regional and international conventions on the marine environment to which the Government of Trinidad and Tobago is a party. The five issues identified as critical are hydrocarbon mining, marine transportation, fisheries resources exploitation, coastal erosion, and coastal development. The first three are located within the nearshore and offshore marine environment. The other two remaining issues are associated with land contiguous to the coastline and within the land–water interface. Each is discussed below. Human activities in these areas affect the coastal and marine environments in specific ways. Figure 5.2 highlights the coastal/marine environmental hotspots as a result of human activities. Use of the marine environment is likely to increase in the future and, given the current level of pollution, it is essential that monitoring of resources be systematically pursued to mitigate current problems and prevent or reduce potential ones.
