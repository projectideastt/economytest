In the same survey, an examination of coastal development projects completed during the last 25 years indicates that 55% of the projects undertaken in coastal areas along Trinidad’s west coast are of an industrial or commercial nature. The size of coastal wetlands along the west coast has decreased over the last 25 years but the wetland communities of Caroni and Oropouche have remained in place even though intense physical coastal development is within closer proximity.

5.6.3 Condition and Trends The trend in coastal development in Trinidad and Tobago is likely to remain dictated by existing conditions: industrialisation on Trinidad’s west coast and tourism on Tobago south west coast. In Trinidad the development of new ports and harbours with the attendant engineering works are required to support the growing energy industry; and in Tobago small scale tourism is being encouraged by State agencies. This tourism form includes a dependence on beaches and water sports as an attraction. 5.6.4 Response The main administrative approach to physical development remains the development planning approval process and the CEC process. Both these processes include the conduct of EIAs and other studies to ensure completeness and are supported by various rules and guideline set out through legislation or regulation. The coast and coastline remain within the larger responsibility of physical development. There are no known plans or policies being considered specifically for coastal development.
