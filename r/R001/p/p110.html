In 2005, WASA commissioned its waste water treatment plant in Beetham which is designed to treat a maximum of 180 million litres of waste water daily, generated by a population of approximately 365,000 (Water Technology 2006). The facility is described as environmentally friendly, incorporating “the latest in ultra violet technology for disinfection and includes anaerobic digestion and biogas removal systems” (Grimes 2005). A few years ago In response to a government mandate, WASA took control of the maintenance of 24 waste water systems originally organised by the NHA and subsequently considered taking responsibility for maintenance of 150 private wastewater treatment plants that are either malfunctioning or essentially abandoned by the private developers (Grimes 2005). Some attempts were made by WASA to provide guidelines for repair and restoration of the treatment systems prior to take over. However, this process has not realised the desired results.
