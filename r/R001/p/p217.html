LEGAL SERVICES DEPARTMENT

Nariva Swamp was finally designated as an Environmentally Sensitive Area pursuant to the Environmentally Sensitive Areas Rules, 2001. The effect of the designation is that the Nariva Swamp is deemed a Managed Resource Protected Area where there are prescribed limitations on the use and activities within the swamp. The Authority can effectively take enforcement action against anyone who contravenes provisions in the notice. Such action can take the form of what is termed breach of an “environmental requirement” whereby the Notice of Violation process is initiated. It is also important to note that under section 70(2) of the Environmental Management Act, No. 03 of 2000 that:

“any person who knowingly or recklessly undertakes or conspires to allow any activity in an “environmentally sensitive area” or with respect to an “environmentally sensitive species” designated under section 41, which may have an adverse impact on the environment within such area or on such species, commits an offence and is liable, on conviction on indictment, to a fine of one hundred thousand dollars and imprisonment for two years.”

Therefore, Criminal action can also be effected.

TECHNICAL SERVICES DEPARTMENT

The Strategic Priorities of the Environmental Management Authority (EMA) are Clean Air, Clean Water, Waste Management, Noise Management and Healthy Ecosystems. Based on these, the EMA’s work plan for each year is developed. In addition, the Enabling Strategies to support the work of the EMA and to facilitate the achievement of the strategic priorities are Communication, Networking, Organization Development, Systems/Legislative Review and Coordination /Government Relations.

The EMA’s Technical Services Department (TSD) implements environmental projects of a technical nature based on the above strategic priorities. In addition, TSD executes operational functions such as processing Certificate of Environmental Clearance (CEC) applications (under the Certificate of Environmental Clearance Rules, 2001), processing Noise Variations (under the Noise Pollution Rules, 2001), designating Environmentally Sensitive Areas and Species (under the Environmentally Sensitive Areas Rules, 2001 and Environmentally Sensitive Species Rules, 2001), and responding to environmental incidents and complaints.

Establishment of EMA – South Office The Department was physically split in 2005 in two geographic locations, with the majority of staff in the head office in Port-of-Spain and a satellite office established in San Fernando. This was done to allow the officers quicker access to the EMA’s clients in South Trinidad and conversely to allow persons living in South Trinidad closer access to basic services offered by the EMA such as collecting application forms for CECs and Noise Variations, receiving said forms from the public and its help desk services.
