7.3.3 Current Status and Trends Little work has been done on the ecological status of many individual species and as such Trinidad and Tobago needs to embark on an assessment of the status and numbers of its species for better resource management. Nevertheless, a comprehensive revision of the checklist of the flora of Trinidad and Tobago was conducted during 2002 by the National Herbarium and Kew Gardens, England. To date, there are over 2,500 recorded plant species in Trinidad and Tobago in about 175 families (Table 7.8). There are 2,160 flowering plant species40, 91 of which are endemic to Trinidad and 21 are endemic to Tobago. Of the plant species recorded, there are some 845 introduced and naturalised plant species which account for about 24 percent of the total number of vascular plants. Trinidad and Tobago is believed to have as many as 10 times the amount of flora as fauna. To date over 1,000 animal species has been discovered. Table 7.8 illustrates the present knowledge about the number of faunal species in Trinidad and Tobago. Except in the case of domestic and farmed animals, there are limited records of the introduced and naturalised fauna. There are several possible fauna that are considered endemic, but further studies are required to conclusively prove that these species do not, in fact, exist elsewhere.
