Chapter 3: Atmosphere

3.1 Overview The Earth is surrounded by a blanket of gases (the atmosphere), which starts at ground level and stretches some 800 km above the planet, becoming thinner until it gradually reaches space. Essentially, the atmosphere is comprised of several layers which are differentiated by their thermal characteristics, chemical composition, and density (Figure 3.1). Life on Earth is supported by the atmosphere, solar energy, and the planet’s magnetic fields. The atmosphere absorbs the energy from the sun; recycles carbon, nitrogen, water and other chemicals; and works with the electrical and magnetic forces to provide a moderate climate. The atmosphere also protects humans, animals, and plants from high-energy radiation; the frigid vacuum of space; and from drifting rocks, chunks of metal and other bits and pieces of space debris that occasionally collide with Earth.
