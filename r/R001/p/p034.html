Chapter 2: Introduction to the Main Drivers of Change Affecting Trinidad and Tobago

2.1 Introduction The Central Statistical Office’s 2005 mid-year estimates puts the population of Trinidad and Tobago at over 1.29 million (CSO 2005a), Trinidad and Tobago is a mix of ethnicities and cultures, predominantly of African and Indian ancestry. As the southernmost islands in the Caribbean archipelago, the country consists of 2 larger islands with a total land area of 5,128 km2, and 21 smaller islands and islets, with the more notable 5 islands off Trinidad’s north-western coast and the 3 key islands off Tobago’s south-central coast. Its economic jurisdiction goes beyond its terrestrial boundaries with a claimed EEZ in 2003 extending to some 60,659 km2. Its uniqueness from the rest of the Caribbean Islands is reflected in its mineralogy and biodiversity which mirrors those of the neighbouring South American mainland.

In the development of Trinidad and Tobago, the livelihoods of its people and their cultures are based primarily on the utilisation of its natural resources.
