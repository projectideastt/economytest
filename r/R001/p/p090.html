The utilisation of natural gas is likely to increase as more gas-based companies are expected to be established in Trinidad’s south-west over the short term. Some of these companies will use the natural gas as feedstock in their production processes or for the generation of electrical energy. Utilisation of natural gas increased from 2,574 mcm in 1999 to 11,789 mcm in 2003 (Ministry of Finance 2004). LNG consumption increased from 4,397 mcm in Fiscal Year 1999/2000 to 11,789 mcm in Fiscal Year 2002/03. By the first nine months of Fiscal Years 2003/04, consumption had recorded 10,759 mcm. Additionally vented natural gas decreased over the period 1999 to 2004 from 930 mcm to 271 mcm. While there was fluctuation over the period, there was a discernable downward trend especially from Fiscal Year 2001/2002 when 1,017 mcm was vented. The demand for natural gas is growing for both industrial and domestic uses. The proposed construction of five industrial estates eTecK and three by the NEC as well as expansion of Point Lisas Industrial Estate will require tremendous electricity which is likely to be fuelled by natural gas. Additionally, the new industries proposed by NEC are described as gas-based industries and are expected to utilise natural gas as feedstock in the production processes (Table 4.10).
