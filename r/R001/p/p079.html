Thus, ribbon development is to be seen along the Western and Eastern Main Roads in north Trinidad; the Southern Main Road in central Trinidad; the Naparima/Mayaro and Guaracara/ Tabaquite Roads in south Trinidad; and Milford Road in south-west Tobago. The East-West Corridor, which in north Trinidad spans a distance of some 48 km, epitomises this continuous form of linear urban development. Data from the CSO for the period 1960 to 1990 (CSO 1995) showed decreasing populations for the cities of Port of Spain and San Fernando, and an increase in population for the borough of Arima. In 2000, the population of Chaguanas was the highest of all areas censored (CSO 2000). During 1960 to 1990 there was movement towards suburban living as populations relocated to the new housing subdivisions constructed on green field sites on the periphery of the core urban centres of both Port of Spain and San Fernando, leading to the extended urban areas identified as greater Port of Spain, and the San Fernando sub-region.
