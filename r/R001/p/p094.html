Currently, land used for agriculture is under threat since the best agricultural lands – those in agricultural land capability I to III – are principally located in the valleys of the Northern Range and have been encroached upon by uses such as housing, business places, road construction and sport fields. Areas such as Diego Martin, Maraval, Santa Cruz, St Joseph/Maracas for example are all major residential areas. 4.4.2 Pressure Pressure has been brought to bear on the land through agricultural practices which are at variance with appropriate land uses and sustainable land management practices. In Trinidad and Tobago, these practices have taken forms such as squatting; slash and burn land clearance methods; farming in inappropriate areas such as on steep slopes and protected forested areas; increasing use of pesticides and fertilisers; loss of agricultural land to other uses such as housing, industry and roads; and transformation of a large section of former Caroni lands8 into small parcels for intensive agriculture. Some of the impacts from these practices include loss of good agricultural land as well as land degradation.
