CHAIRMAN’S MESSAGE

For the 2005 reporting period the EMA’s Board of Directors operated under the Chairmanship of Dr. John Agard. On behalf of the current Board of Directors it is my pleasure to present the 2005 Annual Report. This report reflects the EMA’s ongoing commitment to continually improve environmental protection and sustainable resource management in Trinidad and Tobago. On behalf of the Board of the Environmental Management Authority (EMA) it is my pleasure to present the 2005 Annual Report. We achieved a milestone in this reporting year: The EMA completed public consultations on the revision of the National Environmental Policy (NEP) and forwarded its recommendations to the Government. The revised NEP was submitted to the Trinidad and Tobago Cabinet in September 2005.

The EMA remains committed to using the best tools available to report on the state of the nation’s environment. This 2005 Report departs from the single theme focus of past reports and examines the state of several aspects of the environment, which allows reporting of a complex topic in a more appropriate manner. Significant effort continues to be made towards the acquisition of data that can be used to monitor and track changes in the environment. Trinidad and Tobago is the most industrialised country in the Commonwealth Caribbean, and through the use of the EMA’s subsidiary legislation, we have continued to work towards our goal of healthy ecosystems and protection of our rich biological diversity through the designation of three Environmentally Sensitive Species and one Environmentally Sensitive Area. While we celebrate our successes in environmental management, we realise that our environment is being affected by accelerated development activities and there is much more work to be done. During 2005 the EMA committed to pursuing the required subsidiary environmental management legislation, including the necessary amendments to the Water Pollution Rules, the draft Air Pollution Rules and the draft Deposit Refund Bill. These pieces of legislation will ensure a strong system of environmental and human health protection. Mr. Shyam Dyal Deputy Chairman January 2013
