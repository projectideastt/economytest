In addition, the old idea of utilising water taxis between Port of Spain and San Fernando has re-emerged as another possible solution. 4.3 Mining 4.3.1 Description Mining in Trinidad and Tobago is pursued for the recovery of hydrocarbons and minerals. Figure 4.2 highlights the economic geology of Trinidad and Tobago. The country has commercial deposits of asphalt, oil and natural gas, which constitute its petroleum sector and which have been exploited over the years. Mineral deposits, mostly aggregates and clays, have been mined historically for use principally in the construction industry. Mining techniques utilised in the extraction of the resources include well drilling operations for oil and natural gas and open pit mining for asphalt and aggregate. Asphalt and oil have been exploited since 1908, and by the 1960’s were among the most significant contributors to the country’s foreign exchange earnings. Land-based exploitation became less critical as exploration and operation migrated towards the marine environment, particularly in southwest and southeast Trinidad. Only within the last 25 years has natural gas been increasingly exploited as an export product and for feedstock for other industries.
