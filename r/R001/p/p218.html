National Ambient Air Quality Monitoring Network Equipment was procured and set up to measure carbon monoxide, sulfur and nitrogen oxides, ground level ozone and meteorological parameters in Point Lisas. Personnel from EMA and PLIPDECO were trained to maintain the equipment and collect data with ROSE Environmental Limited providing back-end support. This project represented the preliminary phase in establishing a National Ambient Air Quality Monitoring Network and has enabled technical capacity building in the field of air quality monitoring for those involved. The result was and continues to be the collection of continuous time-series ambient air quality data from the most industrialized part of Trinidad and Tobago. In addition, the EMA acquired a Particulate Matter (PM) Analyzer, which allowed for the collection of a more complete data set thus allowing for the calculation of air quality indices, which was not previously possible in the absence of PM data.

The EMA was able to do a preliminary assessment of the quality of the ambient air at that location and characterise air pollution present.

Vehicle Exhaust Emissions Standards One of the EMA’s strategic objectives under Clean Air is the incorporation of vehicle exhaust emission standards for the exhaust emissions of the various classes of motor vehicles into legally enforceable regulations.

The committee established by the EMA to Phase Out the Use of Leaded Fuel was retained to make recommendations for the implementation of the vehicle emission standards. It analysed various possibilities and prepared a report, which identified options for the implementation of the said standards in June 2005, this was sent to the Ministry of Public Utilities and the Environment for further action.

Draft Water Pollution Rules The Draft Water Pollution Rules were developed by the Environmental Management Authority pursuant to guidelines contained in the Environmental Management Act, 2000, the National Environmental Policy and the Waste Pollution Management Programme.

In accordance with the requirements of Section 52(1) of the Act, the EMA conducted the Pollution Inventory Study in 1997, which provided a detailed assessment of existing significant sources of water pollution in Trinidad and Tobago. The Pollution Inventory Study, together with other reports, provided the basis for the development of a comprehensive Register of Water Pollutants in accordance with Section 52(2); this forms the First Schedule of the Water Pollution Rules, as well as the maximum Permissible Levels of water pollutants, which form the Second Schedule of the Water Pollution Rules. In 2005, the Legislative Review Committee of the Ministry of the Attorney General requested that international benchmarks be included to justify the Second Schedule of the Draft Water Pollution Rules. This information was forwarded to the Committee and as at December 2005, the EMA was awaiting further instructions.
