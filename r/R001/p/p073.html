The EMA has established the National Ozone Unit (NOU) which has established a quota system and a reliable licensing and enforcement system for importers of ODS. To support its effort, the EMA has developed a communication strategy to inform the public and to bring continuous public awareness about ODS and the implementation of the Montreal Protocol. The Government has also taken steps towards achieving its compliance targets (Table 3.8). From the beginning of 2008, Trinidad and Tobago has made the commitment to no longer import CFCs, which will be two years ahead of most of the Article 5 countries. The National Halon Bank Management Plan was developed in 2001 by the EMA (EMA 2001c) with the assistance of the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP). Halons are utilised in fixed and portable fire protection equipment by various stakeholders which include insurance companies, fire equipment owners, halon users and owners and government ministries (Custom and Excise Department, the protective services, etc).
