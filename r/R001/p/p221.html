PLANS FOR 2006

CORPORATE RELATIONS AND PUBLIC EDUCATION DEPARTMENT

Schools’ Programmes  The 8th annual Secondary Schools’ ‘Hands-on’ Competition and the Secondary Schools’ Public Speaking Competition will be launched in January 2006. The Corporate Relations and Public Education department will also work with the Tobago House of Assembly to launch these programmes in Tobago.  The department will also be expanding its schools’ programme by inaugurating more environmental clubs in both primary and secondary schools.

Media Campaigns  Media campaigns will utilise television documentaries, print and radio advertisements and will focus on the CEC process, Water Pollution issues, as well as an ongoing Anti-Litter Campaign.  Documentaries as well as features on the secondary and primary schools’ competitions will be produced in collaboration with Gayelle - The Channel.

Public Awareness  The department will continue to mount displays that highlight projects under the organisation’s strategic plan, as well as on commemorative days.  In 2006, special emphasis will be placed on educating the public about designated areas and species under the ESS and ESA Rules.

Collaborative Projects  The department will continue to serve on the Disaster Management Committee of the Ministry of Public Utilities and the Environment. The goal of this committee is to ensure that the Ministry is ready to deal with disasters through the development of a disaster management programme that will outline the roles and responsibilities of all the agencies that fall under its purview.  Collaboration will continue with the Ministry of Community Development, Culture and Gender Affairs on the Environment and Sanitation competition under the Prime Minister’s Best Village Competition.  Public education and outreach initiatives on Article 6 issues of the UNFCCC. (Article 6 on the Convention deals with Education, Public Awareness and Training.) This will be done in partnership with the MACC project, under the Caribbean Community Climate Change Centre in Belize.

Volunteer Programme The Volunteer Programme, which was launched in January 2004, will be expanded in 2006 to include additional volunteers. These individuals will act as arms of the EMA in the dissemination of information, the expansion of education and public awareness programmes and as support personnel.
