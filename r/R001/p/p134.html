6.3 highlights the surface water quality in Trinidad and Tobago, based on the study conducted by DHV Consultants et al. in 1999. Agricultural chemicals used in both domestic (vegetable and food crop cultivation) and export farming migrate to soils and water courses especially during heavy showers after spraying. Sediment loads have increased in the water courses as a consequence of a number of inappropriate human activities including agriculture, residential construction and quarrying, all of which have been discussed in Chapter Four.
