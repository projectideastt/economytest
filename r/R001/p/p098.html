Loss of Agricultural Land Agricultural land is being lost to other uses. Housing, industry and associated recreation and roads are being developed on a number of sites that were former Caroni lands. These include 1,375 ha for housing, 579 ha for industry and an unknown quantity for roads, stadia and other facilities (Table 4.18). Of the approximately 25,472 ha of Caroni land once under agricultural production, approximately 9,862 ha or 39% have been reallocated to small scale farming on one-ha plots and residential, industrial and miscellaneous uses, such as road construction and recreation. While non-agricultural uses accounted for upwards of 791 ha or about 3% of land alienated from former Caroni lands, the conversion of large blocks of sugar cane land into one-ha parcels for vegetable and food crop farming covers 16 sites and extends over an area of 8,270 ha. Moreover, given inadequate levels of monitoring of agricultural lands and the fact that there is limited enforcement to restrict use of leased agricultural land for other purposes, it can be expected that some of these holdings over time will be converted to non-agricultural use. 4.4.3 Current State and Trends Land used in agriculture has declined over the years and this is reflected in the total number of agricultural parcels, the land area under cultivation and the number of holders of agricultural parcels. Based on the 2004 Agricultural Census the number of agricultural parcels was recorded at 26,131, but this was a reduction of some 15,611 parcels or 37.4% from 1982 when the number of parcels recorded was 41,743.
