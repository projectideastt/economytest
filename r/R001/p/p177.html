 Wetlands and coastal ecosystems and species – Sea level rise will affect wetlands especially those in Trinidad through salt water intrusion which would change the vegetation. Already salt water intrusion is a problem in some wetlands and coastal lowlands such as Bejucal and Felicity in central Trinidad, and in the lower and middle sections of the Oropouche Lagoon in the south. In both regions salt water intrusion was exacerbated by the deterioration of irrigation and drainage systems associated with the production of rice and other crops, thus resulting in saline soil conditions and a change in the ecology of the areas. Sea level rise could severely affect the Oropouche Lagoon where the salt water intrusion impacts were greatest, and thus reverse the current recovery of the lagoon achieved through major repairs in the area undertaken three to four years ago.
