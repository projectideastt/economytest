Coasts and Oceans The coast is the part of the land adjoining or near the ocean/sea. It is often described as the land-water interface. Oceans are defined as the areas of sea or seabed which are under the jurisdiction of Trinidad and Tobago and include its exclusive economic zone (EEZ). Inland Waters Surface and subterranean waters (rivers, streams, lakes, dams, aquifers) and their ecosystems. Biodiversity The number and variety of different organisms in the ecological complexes in which they naturally occur. Organisms are arranged at many levels, ranging from complete ecosystems to the biochemical structures that are the molecular basis of heredity. Thus, the term encompasses different ecosystems, species and genes that must be present for a healthy environment. Economy, Industry, Resource Use This Chapter discusses socio-economic trends and activities as they relate to natural resource uses. 1.7 Format of the 2005 ASOE The 2005 ASOE comprises eight chapters. This chapter – Chapter 1 - is an introductory chapter which provides the rationale for the approach used in this report. Chapter 2 provides an overview of some of the underlying causes or driving forces for environmental change in Trinidad and Tobago. This is done in an attempt to unearth some of the societal conditions driving human actions and their consequential impact on the environment. Chapters 3 through to 8 report on the various thematic areas. Each component identifies a number of issues which are explored in some depth through appropriate indicators to arrive at the state of the resource, the pressures placed on it, and the societal response to its condition.
