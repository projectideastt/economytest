Private initiatives include the 10-year ‘No Net Loss Forest Loss’ Reforestation project being undertaken by the NGC, in collaboration with communities, Government and NGOs, to reforest degraded areas in the Rochard Douglas and Mayaro/Victoria Forests and Morne L’Enfer Forest Reserves. In all, over 315 ha are being targeted for reforestation and rehabilitation. Fire Protection Key among the measures for maintaining forests are those geared at protecting the forests from fires. The Forestry Division and the NRWMP take the lead in their efforts at fire prevention which include the cutting of fire traces, and public education and outreach. Coastal and Marine Protected Areas The Buccoo Reef Marine Park remains the only marine area set aside for protection in Trinidad and Tobago, The reef has an area of 700 ha (0.012 % of Trinidad and Tobago’s total EEZ), suggesting that an insufficient proportion of the ecological components of the country’s marine environment are protected, and as such they remain highly vulnerable to the impacts of human activities. The EMA plans to designate the reef complex as an ESA. The Speyside Reef, though not legally zoned as protected, has been receiving special attention, including the preparation of a management plan and monitoring of the health of the zone. By the end of 2005, three wetlands, one in Tobago and two in Trinidad, had been designated as Ramsar Sites (Table 7.7). Altogether, they cover some 15,919 ha. Natural Resource Management Conservation and protection of species habitat requires the Government and the people of Trinidad and Tobago to engage in systems which would allow for sustainable management of biodiversity. A strategy and an action plan developed for Trinidad and Tobago as part of its obligations to the UNCBD provide a number of initiatives for environmental education, policy rationalisation and reform, legislative reform and enforcement, strengthening of research and information management capacity, and for institutional strengthening.
