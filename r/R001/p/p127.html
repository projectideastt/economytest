Insofar as development projects are concerned, the State is engaged in the erection of coastal defence structures in communities such as Guayaguayare, (where approximately 100 m of coastal defence were constructed) and Cap de Ville. These efforts are supplemented by the coastal defence projects of private property owners. Monitoring remains at the limited project level identified earlier with no published attempts to design or implement any new national coastal monitoring scheme.

5.6 Coastal Development 5.6.1 Description Like many developing island states, where land resources and difficult topography are significant factors, coastal development levels are high on those parts of the coastline that lie on the leeward side of the island. On the island of Trinidad, the western side adjacent to the Gulf of Paria has undergone such transformations. On Tobago, the leeward side is the northern side adjacent to the Caribbean Sea. Development on Trinidad for traditional reasons is significantly higher because of increased opportunities, the presence of a well-developed oil and gas industry and other resources. The coastal development form on the West Coast is varied and includes recreational, commercial, industrial, residential, and open use. Coastal development on the other coastlines is significantly less, due to restriction imposed by lack of access and steep or difficult topography. In Tobago recent coastal development is concentrated on the southwestern coast and comprises mainly small hotels and tourism facilities. 5.6.2 Pressure Over the last few years, high income residential and industrial use has been at the forefront of coastal development. The Port of Spain to Chaguaramas coastline is particularly subject to high levels of commercial and high income residential developments that not only reshape the local coastline but may be forcing changes to coastal circulation, coastal water quality and nearby drainage. Surveys conducted in 200416 indicate that on this 25 km stretch of coastline only 14% is in use as open space while approximately 40% is currently used for industrial or commercial use.
