MESSAGE FROM THE MANAGING DIRECTOR/CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER

I am pleased to present the Environmental Management Authority’s (EMA) 2005 Assessment of the State of the Environment Report (ASOE). It is my belief that closer collaboration and more effective communication with our internal and external stakeholders are crucial to the delivery of the environmental targets established for the Authority. In 2005, the EMA explored new and creative ways to achieve its targets and worked towards them by establishing systems to ensure access to the best available scientific and technological information; determining which problems pose the most immediate and critical risks to our natural environment, human health and ultimately the quality of life available to citizens. On the operational front, the EMA established a new office in La Romain allowing it to better serve the public of south Trinidad. Members of the public became able to access the EMA’s services at locations in north Trinidad, south Trinidad and Tobago. The EMA continued to record steady progress in monitoring and protecting the environment. Trinidad and Tobago’s first ambient air quality monitoring station was commissioned at Pt. Lisas. The station is equipped to continuously monitor carbon monoxide, sulphur and nitrogen oxides, ground level ozone, particulate matter and meteorological parameters. This project represented the preliminary phase in establishing a National Ambient Air Quality Monitoring Network and has enabled technical capacity building in the field of air quality monitoring for those involved. The result was and continues to be the collection of continuous time-series ambient air quality data from the most industrialized part of Trinidad and Tobago. The EMA continued its groundwater quality-monitoring programme at 32 wells and boreholes throughout Trinidad and Tobago. This field survey revealed contamination of groundwater from gasoline stations in Maraval, in Charlieville in Central Trinidad as well as Scarborough and Canaan in Tobago. Some of the Water and Sewerage Authority's potable water producing catchments also showed signs of contamination. Those catchments were in Four Roads in the Diego Martin Producing Field; in Curepe in the Valsayn Producing Field and at Don Miguel Road, which is part of the El Socorro Gravels. By designating the Manatee, Pawi and Sabrewing Hummingbird as Environmentally Sensitive Species, the EMA continued to advance its mandate to protect key species. Legal Notices were signed on World Environment Day - June 5, and gazetted on June 17, Legal Supplement Part B – Vol.44, No. 98 – June 2005. The EMA has also been very busy in fulfilling its mandate to educate the public about the nation’s environmental issues through awareness programmes. In 2005, there were many collaborative projects undertaken with our sister agencies. These included an Anti-Litter campaign with TIDCO; a Forest Fire Awareness and Prevention programme with the Forestry Division; the Prime Minister’s Best Village Environment and Sanitation Competition with the Ministry of Community Development and Gender Affairs and Tidy T&T.
