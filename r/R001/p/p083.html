the last two years, the Ministry of Housing through its NHA/HDC has constructed approximately 4,000 housing units per year. The private sector has also been engaged in the programme of housing construction but its production is not publicly documented. Industrial development is also proceeding with the construction of a new industrial park at Union Village, La Brea and expansion of the Point Lisas Industrial Estate and the Atlantic Liquified Natural Gas facility at

Point Fortin. Additionally, the Evolving TecKnologies and Enterprise Development Company Limited (eTecK) recently commissioned the design and development of several industrial sites in San Fernando, Couva and Piarco while the National Energy Corporation (NEC) is proposing the development of an industrial estate at Chatham. Much of these activities are taking place or are planned to take place in the more urbanised western section of the country. The heavy gas-based industries in particular are to be located along the western coast of Trinidad from Caroni to Point Fortin. Densification of residential development on existing urban land is also intensifying as formerly single family housing on relatively large plots of land are currently being used for medium and high rise town houses, condominiums and apartment complexes. This is to be seen in private developments in areas such as The Buoys in Dhein’s Bay, La Riviera in Westmoorings, and Bayside Towers in Cocorite. Hillside development is also extending on southern flanks of the Northern Range as they accommodate development of apartment complexes and townhouses in addition to palatial single family houses and unauthorised single-unit residential structures further uphill. 4.2.4 Response The State enacted the Squatter Regularisation Act to regularise unofficial development on some 250 sites and its Land Settlement Agency (LSA) is currently attempting to enforce the anti squatting law given its publicly stated policy of zero tolerance on squatting. Additionally, the LSA is working to improve the squatter communities as its mandate dictates (See Section 4.3.4). The State Lands Act and its new amendment are intended to discourage illegal occupation or unauthorised use of publicly held land. However, enforcement continues to be sporadic and ineffective. Decentralisation has been spoken of over the years and some attempt was made to decentralise some Government services to regional locations. More needs to be done to effect a significant reduction in demand for transportation services.
