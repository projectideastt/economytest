6.3.4 Response Ground water provides an important source of the nation’s potable supply and therefore requires protection from leachates, creeping development, denuded hillsides in water recharge areas and over use. Several of these issues are dealt with elsewhere, including in the section on surface water in this chapter as well as chapters on Land and Biodiversity. The coastal aquifer in Port of Spain, formed from the Port of Spain Gravels, is approximately 91.4 m (300 feet) deep and very porous. In this aquifer system, WASA has noted that “… groundwater pollution, salt water intrusion and over-pumping continue to be serious concerns” (Water Technology 2006). The reforestation programme is expected to assist in greening the hillsides and thus improve the quality of the recharge areas. Implementation of the Water Pollution Rules is expected to discourage the indiscriminate discharge of untreated effluents into the nation’s waterways on the one hand, and encourage cooperation between environmentally polluting enterprises and the appropriate agencies to reduce or eliminate the type and concentration of the effluent to the standards set under the new Water Pollution Rules, on the other. 6.4 Aquatic Ecosystems-Wetland Health 6.4.1 Description Trinidad and Tobago has a large number of wetlands. According to the Wetlands Committee, approximately 71 wetland sites have been identified and the inventory is incomplete35. The wetlands consist of over 25,000 ha, comprising swamps, lagoons and marshes. Many are located close to or on the coasts of both islands. Many wetland sites offer a combination of swamps, lagoons and marshes with a range of flora and fauna. The IMA identified and mapped 42 wetland sites in Trinidad and 13 in Tobago with a range of sizes (Table 6.6). It was found that the wetlands range in type and complexity (IMA 1999). There is the single item fringe mangrove in Scotland Bay, the freshwater swamp forest in Maracas, the estuarine mangrove in Manzanilla, and the wet pasture in Lucy Vale. The more diverse systems are the freshwater marsh/freshwater swamp forest/ estuarine mangrove/palm forest of Nariva, the lagoon fringe/basin mangrove/freshwater pond of the Bon Accord Lagoon/Buccoo Bay, and the marshes/mangrove swamp/brackish and saline lagoons/tidal mud flats of Caroni.
