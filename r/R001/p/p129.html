Chapter 6: Inland Waters

6.1 Overview 6.1.1 The Rivers Trinidad and Tobago have a myriad of rivers and swamps which have been identified and mapped. Less well known are the lakes, ponds, and reservoirs and water impoundment areas. Most of the inland waters are located in Trinidad and the river systems are defined by the country’s topography. The Northern Range which runs from the north western tip of Trinidad due east across to Toco in the north east contains two characteristically different river systems. The natural drainage channels on the northern side of the Northern Range that discharge into the Caribbean Sea are short, steep, narrow, gorge-like valleys. However, the Northern Range’s southern and landward slopes are less steep and many of the large rivers in Trinidad have their sources on the southern slopes. The Santa Cruz/San Juan, Maracas/St Joseph, Caura/Tacarigua, Arouca, Oropuna, Guanapo, El Mamo, Aripo, Maraval, St Anns/Port of Spain, Diego Martin and Chaguaramas Rivers originate on the southern slopes of the Northern Range. With the exception of the last four rivers, the other south-draining rivers mentioned above join together to form the Caroni River which extends for 40 km and empties into the Gulf of Paria. The main drainage channel on the east coast is the North Oropouche River which receives, as tributaries from the Northern Range, the Rio Grande, Melajo and Guaico Rivers. The Central Range runs diagonally from northeast to southwest of the island and consists mainly of hill-ridges radiating from rugged limestone peaks. River systems originating in this range flow northward to both west and east and southward to both west and east. The main rivers with their source on the northern side of the Central Range are: the Arena, Talparo, Tumpuna and Cumuto Rivers, which are tributaries of the Caroni River; and the Cunapa and Sangre Grande Rivers, which are tributaries of the North Oropouche River. The L’Ebranche River empties on the east coast and the Couva River on the west coast. The main rivers which arise on the southern flanks are the Guaracara and Poole Rivers, together with a series of smaller rivers which drain into the Nariva Swamp.
